We built this website to help you find all the resources needed to achieve one of your dreams of owning a home. However, there is a a fine line between dreams and nightmares. Here are some pro-tips to help you decide whether buying a house is the right decision or perhaps you want to consider chasing other financial dreams. Great tips below and more life resources can be found on lifehacker .


  • Tip #1

    Don’t think of a home as your main investment

    Most experts agree: while buying a house is an investment, it’s not a great investment. So if this is your only basis for buying a home, it’s probably not the best one.

  • Tip #2

    Opportunity cost of renting vs. buying

    Sometimes, you can actually earn more money over time by renting and investing than buying. The New York Times has a useful interactive calculator that considers all of this. Plug in all of your details—home price, interest rate, housing growth rate, closing costs, rental costs, etc.—and you’ll get a detailed view of what the costs and opportunity costs are.

  • Tip #3

    Consider your total net worth and save up

    Your home should not be your primary asset. You shouldn’t give up your entire savings, especially your retirement savings, just to become a homeowner. You should have a healthy emergency fund saved before buying a home and prepare savings for other expenses that will inevitably pop up—maintenance, decorations, improvements and so on.

  • Tip #4

    Avoid being “house poor”

    You don’t want all of your net worth to be tied up in your home, and that’s what being "house poor" is—when you can’t afford to make ends meet because you’ve spent everything on a house. Don’t forego financial basics and buy a house for emotional reasons or because it’s expected of you.
